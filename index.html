The Principal Consultant

Dr. Sokoyama K.
Songu-Mbriwa, PhD

"I spent 23 years in DC Superior Court watching what the system did to young people — and to the professionals trying to serve them. Fidescope exists because both deserve better."

Dr. Songu-Mbriwa is the founder of Fidescope Consulting Group and a practitioner-scholar with over two decades of direct experience in the District of Columbia juvenile justice system. Beginning as a Juvenile Probation Officer in DC Family Court in 2004 and rising to Supervisory Probation Officer, he brings a level of field authority that no textbook alone can produce — the authority of a man who has sat across from justice-involved youth, their families, and the systems that serve them for more than 23 years.

His 2024 doctoral dissertation at Grand Canyon University examined compassion fatigue and burnout among juvenile probation officers — one of the first qualitative studies to address the unique dual law enforcement and social work mandate these professionals carry. That research is the empirical foundation of Fidescope's practitioner wellness consulting practice.

A certified PeopleKeys Behavioral Consultant (CBC) and StudentKeys Specialist, SHRM-CP, and university faculty member at both Bard High School Early College and Prince George's Community College, Dr. Songu-Mbriwa brings the rare combination of street-level practice, doctoral-level research, and classroom-tested pedagogy to every engagement. His consulting work is shaped by his conviction that the professionals who serve justice-involved youth deserve the same quality of support and development that they give to the young people in their care.

The Fidescope
Wellness Index

The Fidescope Wellness Index (FWI) is a multi-profession, non-clinical wellness awareness tool built on the ProQOL backbone — designed to help organizations and practitioners understand the wellness landscape of their people before crisis arrives, not after.

Unlike generic wellness surveys, the FWI is calibrated for the unique occupational demands of four professions that carry extraordinary human burden: juvenile justice workers, K–12 educators, attorneys, and judges. It sees what standard tools miss.

Forthcoming Textbook

Everchanging:
The Book

Under contract with Cognella Academic Publishing, Everchanging: Contemporary Social and Psychological Issues in Juvenile Justice is the first upper-level undergraduate textbook to integrate DISC behavioral assessment and VIA Character Strengths frameworks into a comprehensive treatment of juvenile justice issues.

Drawing on 23+ years of DC Superior Court practice and original doctoral research, the textbook brings practitioner authority to every chapter — from racial and ethnic disparities to trauma-informed practice, alternatives to incarceration, and the compassion fatigue that affects the people who serve this population.

Ch. 9 From Surviving to Thriving: Trauma-Informed Approaches
